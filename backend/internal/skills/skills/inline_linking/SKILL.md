# Inline Linking

## INLINE LINKING, MANDATORY (articles with zero links are unfinished)

An article without hyperlinks looks like it was generated by a chatbot. Links are how you prove
the content is researched, not fabricated. They also drive SEO value and reader trust.

## HARD RULES

- Every article MUST have a minimum of 3-5 inline hyperlinks per 1,000 words.
  A 2,000-word article with zero links is a FAILURE. Period.
- Every major claim backed by a statistic MUST link to the source.
  BAD: "According to a recent study, 73% of marketers now use AI tools."
  GOOD: "According to [HubSpot's 2026 State of Marketing report](URL), 73% of marketers now use AI tools."
- Link text should be descriptive, not generic.
  BAD: [click here](URL), [this article](URL), [source](URL)
  GOOD: [HubSpot's 2026 State of Marketing report](URL), [Google's MCP documentation](URL)
- When you find data during research (webSearch, deepResearch), SAVE THE URLs and use them.
  If a search result gave you a statistic, the link to that source MUST appear in the article.
- Internal links to the user's own content are valuable. If you know their domain and have
  found related pages, link to them naturally in the prose.

## LINK PLACEMENT PATTERNS

- Introduction: 1-2 links to set credibility early (link to the news event, the report, the launch)
- Body sections: 1-2 links per H2 section minimum. Each section should cite at least one source.
- Conclusion: 0-1 links. The CTA or a callback to the most important source.
- Tables and lists: Link items directly when they reference external tools, platforms, or resources.

## WHAT TO LINK

- Statistics and data points -> link to the original report or study
- Named tools, platforms, or products -> link to their homepage or relevant page
- News events or announcements -> link to the original announcement or press coverage
- Quotes -> link to the source where the quote appeared
- Concepts or frameworks -> link to the definitive explanation (MDN, Wikipedia, official docs)
- The user's own related content -> link to their published pages when relevant

## LINK COUNT MINIMUMS

- Under 1,000 words  : minimum 3 links
- 1,000-2,000 words  : minimum 5 links
- 2,000-3,000 words  : minimum 8 links
- 3,000+ words       : minimum 10 links
- Zero links = automatic failure regardless of word count

## OUTBOUND LINK SAFETY

- ONLY use domains from the authoritySources list (from getSourcesInsights())
  or well-known institutional sources (.gov, .edu, major publications)
- NEVER link to competitor domains
- Aim for 3-4 authority site links per article
